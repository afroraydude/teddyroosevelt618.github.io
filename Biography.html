Biography

Theodore Roosevelt was born on October 17, 1858 in New York City. His first political job was a representative at the New York State Assembly, becoming the youngest person to hold the position. Soon after, Roosevelt started to speed through different public service positions, such as Captain of the National Guard and minority leader of the New York State Assembly. He ran for mayor of New York City in 1886 but was sadly defeated. Soon after he reentered his political life and got many more positions. He then became civil service commissioner, then a New York City police commissioner and assistant US navy secretary under William Mckinley. Roosevelt later took a keen interest in the Spanish American War. He joined the Rough Riders and led a bold charge on San Juan Hill, and was later nominated for a medal of honor and was considered a war hero. Then he was elected as governor of New York in 1898. Roosevelt served one year of vice-presidency under William McKinley, until McKinley was assassinated. Roosevelt finished out the term, and then ran for president the next term and won.
